DARIAH Theme project: Contemporary collecting and COVID-19: barriers, bottlenecks, and perspectives in digital curation

Seminar series:
Contemporary Collecting and COVID-19

In 2020, institutions worldwide have started to collect witnesses and memories of the COVID-19 pandemic. A broad range of artefacts (or their digital images), written and oral accounts, digital documents and social media content, etc. have been collected by museums, libraries, web archives, and researchers. The peculiar nature of these contemporary collections, which are often assemblages of physical artefacts and born-digital material, poses many challenges in relation to the methods of collection and accession in cultural heritage organisations, participation and engagement in the formation of these collections, the cataloguing and curation of this heterogenous and abundant data, the standards needed for their digital preservation, their interpretation, the legal and ethical challenges connected to this material (ownership; copyright; data protection; code of ethics).

This series of events aims to bring together practitioners and researchers in the heritage sector and in digital preservation to explore these collections of the pandemic, and the practices activated to collect, preserve, curate, and interpret this material. We envision four interdisciplinary events, in which to discuss perspectives and solutions for the collection

We welcome contributions that address the following non-exhaustive list of topics:

  • What collection strategies were adopted?
  • What methods were used to collect memories of the pandemic?
  • How was a broader participation encourage? Which communities were involved?
  • How did the lockdowns and personal circumstances of the staff affect the collection process (collecting in times of trauma; access to organizational infrastructure and resources)?
  • How did the project deal with legal aspects?
  • What ethical concerns emerged during this project?
  • How was the material accessioned and catalogued (metadata, formats, etc.)?
  • How do these collections contribute to the institution overall mission?
  • How will these collections be preserved?
  • How will the collections be displayed? Will they be available online or presented in exhibitions?
